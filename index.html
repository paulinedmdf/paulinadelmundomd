About

Bridging the clinic, the dataset, and the policy brief.

I'm a physician trained in general and occupational medicine with a Master of Public Health in Epidemiology & Biostatistics from Johns Hopkins. My work sits where clinical care, computational methods, and policy meet: designing real-world evidence studies, building predictive models on EHR and claims data, and translating findings for pharma, biotech, and federal-grant audiences.

Before moving into data science full-time, I practiced in hospital, outpatient, and telehealth settings; led systematic reviews that shaped national oncology guidelines in the Philippines; and drove policy work integrating accurate cause-of-death certification into medical education nationwide. I keep one foot in each world on purpose — the questions worth modeling are the ones clinicians and patients actually live.

  • Real-world evidence

    EHR, claims, and SDoH pipelines benchmarked against the National Inpatient Sample; cohort analyses for pharma and biotech consulting clients.

  • Methods & modeling

    Predictive modeling of mortality and readmission risk, PCA-based SDoH composites, k-means clustering, regression-based imputation, LLM-assisted analytics.

  • Evidence & policy

    PICO-framed systematic reviews, GRADE evidence synthesis, NIH and AHRQ grant writing, and legislative advocacy that changes what gets taught and paid for.
